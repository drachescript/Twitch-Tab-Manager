# Privacy Policy — Twitch Tab Manager
Last updated: 14 March 2026

Summary
Twitch Tab Manager runs locally in your browser to open, organize, and close Twitch tabs based on which followed channels are live, and can also try to resume or unmute managed playback on Twitch pages. We do not collect, sell, or share personal data. The extension does not use analytics, advertising, or developer-controlled servers.

What the extension does
- Reads local configuration from extension storage and optional local import/export files such as config.json and follows.txt.
- Uses Twitch’s Helix API when configured, with Twitch-page fallback/live probing when needed, to determine which channels are live.
- Can temporarily open or reuse the official Twitch Following → Channels page, auto-scroll it, and read followed usernames for the “Fetch My Follows” feature.
- Opens and closes manager-controlled Twitch tabs for live or offline channels.
- Injects small local helper scripts on Twitch pages to detect page/player state and try to resume or unmute managed playback when needed.
- Includes local diagnostics, debug tools, and import/export helpers.

Data we process
- Local-only configuration: values such as client_id, access_token, feature toggles, follows, priority channels, and related settings. These remain on your device unless you choose to export or share them.
- Local runtime state: minimal manager state such as enabled/disabled status, last poll timing, de-duplication state, diagnostics, and manager-owned tab state stored via extension storage.
- Twitch page/API requests: your browser talks directly to Twitch over HTTPS where needed for live checks, follows fetching, or Twitch page interaction.

We do NOT collect
- Account credentials
- Browsing history outside Twitch
- Personal identifiers sent to the developer
- Advertising IDs
- Analytics or telemetry sent to developer-controlled servers
- Any sale or sharing of data with third parties

Permissions (why we need them)
- tabs — open/close manager-controlled Twitch tabs, group them, and prevent duplicates.
- storage — save local settings and minimal runtime state on your device.
- alarms — run periodic background polling in the MV3 service worker.
- scripting — inject small helper scripts into Twitch pages only, to detect status and try to resume/unmute managed playback.
- downloads — let you export your own local extension data, such as follows.txt and config.json.
- cookies — allow Twitch requests on Twitch domains to include your existing Twitch session where needed.
- host_permissions (Twitch domains only) — required to interact with Twitch pages and Twitch APIs used by the extension.

Cookies & local storage
The extension does not set advertising or tracking cookies.

Scope:
- Twitch cookies from twitch.tv and www.twitch.tv only, as handled by the browser for Twitch requests where needed.

Purpose:
- Authenticated Twitch page access where required for features such as fetching from the official Following → Channels page and related Twitch page checks.

No collection:
- We do not store, log, upload, sell, or share cookie contents. Cookies are only attached by the browser to Twitch requests where needed.

No cross-site tracking:
- Cookies are not used for sites other than Twitch.

Local storage:
- Limited extension state is stored locally on your device using browser extension storage.

Downloads
The extension uses the Downloads permission only so you can export your own local data, such as follows.txt and config.json, as browser-saved files. It does not read, inspect, modify, or manage unrelated downloads on your device.

Children’s privacy
This extension is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information.

Security
- Twitch requests use HTTPS.
- Your local settings, follows, and tokens remain on your device unless you choose to export or share them.
- Do not publish your Client Secret or access token publicly; rotate tokens if exposed.

Data retention & deletion
All extension data is stored locally on your device.

To remove it, you can:
- delete exported files such as config.json or follows.txt
- clear the extension’s stored data through the extension’s reset/options flow or browser extension settings
- uninstall the extension

Changes to this policy
This policy may be updated to reflect project improvements or Chrome Web Store requirements. The “Last updated” date will be revised when changes are made.
